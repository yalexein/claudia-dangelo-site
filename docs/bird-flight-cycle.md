# Bird Flight Cycle Notes

For believable photorealistic motion, a single in-flight image is not enough. Use at least 3 matching frames:

- `bird-flight-1.webp`
- `bird-flight-2.webp`
- `bird-flight-3.webp`

## Goal

The wings and tail should visibly change shape during flight while keeping:

- the same species markings
- the same side profile
- the same lens feel
- the same light direction
- the same scale family

## Frame roles

- `bird-flight-1.webp`: wings beginning to lift, tail slightly fanned
- `bird-flight-2.webp`: strongest flap position, chest stretched forward, tail balancing the body
- `bird-flight-3.webp`: wings recovering downward / forward, body still moving laterally

## Important

- Keep the bird readable at small size
- Do not make the wing spread too dramatic
- Keep the body center consistent across the 3 frames so the animation does not jitter
- Keep feet tucked naturally or hidden consistently
- Transparent background only, with no baked shadow
